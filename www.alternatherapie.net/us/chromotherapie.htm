Chromotherapy

 

Overview

combination of the Greek words therap: to use/to care for and chroma: color

This involves casting colored light on a person, usually using a halogen lamp with the desired color filter attached. Colors correspond to vibrations and have different speeds, wavelengths and rhythms; colored light can help reharmonize corporal energy, relieve certain maladies or problems, and even alter a negative state of mind.
The light ray may cover the whole body or be aimed at a specific body part, usually a chakra or acupuncture point. This technique requires regularity and perseverance.
On an everyday basis, colors can be used in our environment: colors of murs, walls, clothing and sheets, gemstones, colored lamps, massages with colored essential oils...

 


Guidelines

Chromotherapy is recommended for:
- treating migraines
- rheumatology
- dermatology (herpes zoster, herpes simplex, burns or postoperative scars)
- treating attention deficit disorders, anxiety and depression.

History

Chromotherapy has been used since the farthest reaches of time. According le dr. Paul Galioughi, to Dr. Paul Galioughi, author of the book Medicine of the Pharaohs, medical treatments using colors were administered in Egypt, where priest doctors treated sick people with colors derived from flowers and precious stones.

Chromotherapy was recognized as a form of complementary and alternative therapy by the WHO in 1976.
