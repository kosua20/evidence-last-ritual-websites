The ICPA is a private association created by friends and colleagues of journalist Jack Lorski in the wake of his murder in Scotland. The ICPA is aimed at helping international police and law departments to bring a swift end to the Phoenix's killing spree.

  • SKL-NETWORK.COM

SKL NETWORK is the agency that Jack Lorski worked for. It decided to go public with the two CD-ROMs received from the Phoenix.

  • LIBERATION.FR

LIBERATION.FR has published several articles on the Phoenix case. Use its search engine to access its archives.

LATEST NEWS

23-09-2006  NEWS TRANSFER
From now on, the ICPA will regularly publish the latest news about the investigation on the website phoenix-investigators.org. We invite you to subscribe as soon as you begin decrypting the DVD-ROM.

23-09-2006  THÉO MAKARIOS JOINS THE ICPA
Théo Makarios is a renowned medieval historian and epigraphist. He joins the ICPA team to provide investigators with all the necessary insights into the historical references contained in the DVD-ROM.

22-09-2006  NEW ICPA WEBSITE
The ICPA has launched the website www.phoenix-investigators.org, which should enable better mutual assistance between different investigators and permanent access to various tools that will facilitate the decryption of the DVD-ROM.

21-09-2006  PUBLICATION OF THE PHOENIX DVD-ROM
The ICPA thanks the many people who responded to its appeal; thanks to them, the DVD-ROM was able to be opened. In agreement with the authorities, the ICPA has decided to make it public today. Its decryption can now begin with the help of all Internet users.

20-09-2006  GÉRY MILANDER JOINS THE ICPA
Géry Milander, aka « Fe256 ,» a white-hat hacker who participated in the first two Phoenix investigations, has joined our team. He's an outstanding developer capable of developing powerful computer tools. With the invaluable help of the testimonies we received following our call for witnesses, his first mission is to unlock the Phoenix DVD.

18-09-2006  CALL FOR WITNESSES
The ICPA has decided to publish a call for witnesses in certain French and foreign newspapers to give itself the best possible chance of decrypting the Phoenix DVD-ROM. Indeed, it could contain valuable clues for investigators.

13-09-2006  ICPA RECEIVES A DVD-ROM OF THE PHOENIX
On September 7, the ICPA received an enigmatic DVD-ROM. This shipment, reminiscent in every way of the case of the black CD-ROM sent to the SKL NETWORK agency in October 2003 (see the article in Libération of October 8, 2003), the ICPA contacted the police authorities. After analyzing the fingerprints and DNA traces taken from the DVD cover, the police were certain: this was a new challenge sent by the Phoenix.

28-08-2006  DAVID MARCUS JOINS THE ICPA
David Marcus, the profiler who distinguished himself during the investigation into the kidnapping of Jack Lorski and Karen Gijman, has just joined our team.

26-07-2006  CREATION OF THE ICPA
Steve Webbing, a staff writer at SKL NETWORK, and several friends and colleagues of Jack Lorski create the International Committee to Arrest the Phoenix (ICPA).

24-07-2006  ANNOUNCEMENT OF THE DEATH OF JACK LORSKI
Jeff Mac Evan, the police lieutenant in charge of the Phoenix investigation, officially announces the death of Jack Lorski, whose body has been formally identified by DNA tests.

17-07-2006  JACK LORSKI'S BODY DISCOVERED
The remains of the horribly mutilated body of Jack Lorski, a staff reporter for SKL NETWORK, were found on the shores of Loch Fyne, near Inveraray, Scotland.
