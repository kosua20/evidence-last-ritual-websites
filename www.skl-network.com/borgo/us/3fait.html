 THE FACTS

Saturday, October 2
Arnaud Ivan opens his mailbox to discover an envelope similar to the one that he received a year ago. The esoteric signs hint at the Phoenix's handiwork. The very same day, Arnaud sends the envelope and its contents to the police department's crime investigation division for analysis.

Monday, October 4
The agency's editorial committee holds a meeting to decide on the best course of action in light of the package received. The possibility of a hoax cannot be ruled out, as countless emails and letters were received from people claiming to be the Phoenix following the announcement of the original black CD-ROM. None were found to be authentic and all were subsequently proven to be the work of disturbed individuals or students with a tasteless sense of humor.
The editorial committee decides to wait for the results of the analyses of the handwriting on the envelope and the DNA tests, and chooses to keep the lid on this new turn of events until the person behind the package is formally identified.

Wednesday, October 13
The crime investigation division returns the results of the analyses. The fingerprints found on the box, the traces of DNA and the handwriting all match those of the Phoenix. There can no longer be any doubt.

Night between October 16 and 17
The Phoenix sends an email to a number of our employees. The police manage to locate where the emails were sent from. Still using the same address - xinoeph@hotmail.com (which the investigators wanted to leave active for the purpose of easily authenticating the killer if he tried to contact them), the Phoenix sent his messages from a cybercafé near Boston (USA).
The message consists of a sequence of digits that has left French police baffled.

Thursday, October 21
Gerd Hanke arrives in Paris. He meets up with members of the Paris police department's crime investigation division and reads up on the latest test results.

Friday, October 22
Gerd Hanke loses no time in deciphering the message sent by the Phoenix and realizes that it refers to Alex Borgo, a young French journalist who took his own life back in February. He immediately gets in contact with Alex's family.

Saturday, October 23
Alex Borgo's mother and sister confirm that the first film shown by the CD-ROM was made by Alex. They give their consent for the film to be broadcast, despite its private nature.

Monday, October 25
In an attempt to get the CD-ROM deciphered more quickly, Gerd Hanke decides to distribute it to the general public. In the afternoon, he organizes a press conference at the French Ministry of the Interior. Ubisoft is called on to duplicate the CD-ROM and distribute the copies.

Tuesday, November 2
An information task force is set up in our agency, in constant contact with Gerd Hanke, to process all information sent in by those involved in the operation.
