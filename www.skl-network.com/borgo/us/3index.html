 PRESS RELEASE

One year to the day after the Jack Lorski case, our agency is once again confronted with the same serial killer that kidnapped our journalist and his assistant.

Early October, we received a new CD-ROM similar to the black one that the self-titled Phoenix sent us last year (see the details of the latest events).

One year ago, the killer obviously took a shrewd decision to keep a low profile, thereby bringing the curtain down on a long series of murders committed in various countries.

The investigators working the case attribute the Phoenix's disappearance to the fact that he had actually reached his objective by slaying Guido Corliano, his 12th and last victim, in the process bringing an end to the strange macabre ritual that fed his murderous impulses.

Since the last known murder, investigators have lost track of the Phoenix. Some voices in the media suggested that he could have taken refuge in Egypt, whereas others mentioned Cuba, though without any evidence to back up their claims.

This case has caused a lot of ink to flow. Following the high level of media coverage, a great deal of criticism was leveled at European police forces for their lack of coordination and teamwork.
It was only back in February that the German serial killer expert, Gerd Hanke, was appointed to head up the European investigation and try to pick up the trail of the Phoenix.

So why has the Phoenix suddenly decided to break cover after all these months of silence (during which he apparently put his murderous activities on hold) by sending us a new CD-ROM?

Investigators fear that the Phoenix's sudden change of attitude could be an ominous sign that a new spate of crimes is on the way. The only exhibit currently in their possession is this CD-ROM, which is why they have called on us to distribute it to the general public, in the hope that it might be "deciphered" to reveal new clues about the killer and his modus operandi.

That is the reason for our going public with this new CD-ROM. We spent a long time thinking about the right opportunity to do so, bearing in mind that the ensuing media coverage is exactly what the killer is looking for. It is not our wish to serve as his instrument or use this case for publicity purposes, contrary to the snide remarks made by certain cynical individuals a few months ago.

It is our intention to make our own contribution in helping to bring this dangerous criminal to justice. Until he is behind bars, we will use all the means at our disposal to help the investigators.
This particular approach proved to be successful last year and helped to secure the release of Jack Lorski and Karen Gijman. We are convinced that we can speed up the investigation by distributing this new exhibit to as many people as possible, and we would like to thank all those who, one way or another, will play a role in bringing an end to the Phoenix's scheming.
