Q.Can I buy cigarettes by international mail?
A.Buying cigarettes by international mail is legal, as long as it is carried out in accordance with international postal regulations and standards: the contents of the parcel must be stated for the destination country postal authorities.

Q.Can cigarettes by international mail be sold without import stamps?
A.Cigarettes can be sold without import stamps and may carry the wording "duty free sales only" providing they are sold from a BONDED WAREHOUSE, like in the case of the cigarettes sold at www.SmokingSeal.com

Q.What is a "bonded warehouse"?
A.This term is used to indicate goods which are not subjected to taxes in the source country so that the taxes, if required, can be applied in the country of destination where the product is used only. www.SmokingSeal.com is one of the first web site to implement BONDED WAREHOUSE sales, in co-operation with the Swiss Customs Authorities.

Q.Can BONDED WAREHOUSE sales be considered a form of DUTY-FREE sales?
A.The term "Duty Free" is improper. A product is "duty free" when it reaches the country of destination where tax may be applied to the discretion of the local Customs Authorities. For this reason, the product may not be "duty free" when it reaches the market. The threshold value from which duty taxes are usually applied varies from country to country, generally in the range from 25 to 50 $US.

Q.Do BONDED WAREHOUSE products have more competitive prices?
A.Yes, unquestionably better. Thanks to the BONDED WAREHOUSE, the product sales price is lower since no taxes are applied. Upon arrival at the destination customs, products are taxed only when their value exceeds a certain threshold (generally in the range from 25 to 50 $US). Sometimes no taxes are applied also when these limits are exceeded. This is because customs officers usually need to deal with an excessive number of products. Products purchased by international mail, provided their value is low, at the current legal state of affairs, are not subject to taxation. This could effect the fiscal system if the amount of these sales was to increase. The new scenario to update international mail laws and regulations cannot be implemented on the short term.
Consequently, BONDED WAREHOUSE sales, while fully respecting international mail laws and regulations, can exploit this transitional phase and make the most of the custom authority's inability to face the new situation. This means that products can be marketed at extremely competitive prices. This is the gateway to a new market, in the full respect of the law.

Q.What is new about BONDED WAREHOUSE sales?
A.Today, the Internet allows you to look for products at the best terms and conditions at online shops world-wide. Logistic and shipping services are offered at increasingly more competitive prices. This is creating the beginning of a global market. Currently, if you live in the USA, for example, you can purchase a taxed product at an online shop based in the UK. In this case, you would pay a higher price than should be expected for a product destined to be exported. In other words, you are paying a tax that should be paid in the US to the British government. If the value of the product exceeds the threshold for duty tax application, the product will additionally be subjected to taxation in the USA and, consequently, you will pay taxes twice, in two different countries.
