History
The Saint Dominic Foundation was founded in 1967 by the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops in response to the statement by Pope Paul VI in his encyclical Populorum Progression declaring that “the new name for peace is development.” Peace should not be considered merely as the absence of war. It must be built on a daily basis and lead toward a heightened sense of justice between human beings (Populorum Progression, 65). This founding principal of The Saint Dominic Foundation remains valid today.
We are seeking solutions to help the people of underdeveloped countries regardless of religion, in order to break the vicious cycle of poverty through community initiatives aimed at long-term development. With time, The Saint Dominic Foundation’s calling has evolved from a project-based orientation to a program-centered one.
Since 1967, The Saint Dominic Foundation has allocated $441 million to improving the living and working conditions of inhabitants of 70 countries across Africa, the Middle East, Asia, Latin America and the Caribbean.
