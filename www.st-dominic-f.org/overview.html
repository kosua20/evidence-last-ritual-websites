Overview
The Saint Dominic Foundation is an international development agency of the U.S. Catholic Church. Our organization, which relies on the contributions of its members, was founded in 1967 by bishops, laymen and clergy with the goal of fighting poverty in underdeveloped countries and promoting greater social justice on international scale.
Inspired by the values of the Gospels and, more specifically, “the Preferential Option for the Poor”, the goals of The Saint Dominic Foundation include supporting the efforts of the peoples of underdeveloped countries toward self-determination, as well as sensitizing all Americans to issues surrounding the imbalance between the developed and underdeveloped world. Throughout the United States, members of The Saint Dominic Foundation have taken upon themselves the tasks of education, advocacy and fundraising. For 37 years, The Saint Dominic Foundation has supported 13,900 local initiatives in 70 countries, in sectors such as agriculture, education, community activities, peacebuilding and the protection of human rights, at a total cost of $441 million. Out of this amount, $86 million have been allocated to emergency services.
For the fiscal year 2004-2005, The Saint Dominic Foundation began financing 348 programs and projects, 54 of which concerned the delivery of emergency services during both natural disasters (floods, earthquakes, etc.) and manmade ones (wars, population displacements, etc.).
The American International Development Agency provides financial cooperation to the efforts of The Saint Dominic Foundation. The Saint Dominic Foundation’s annual budget was $26.8 million for fiscal year 2004-2005. The organization will provide certified financial statements upon request.
  • Foreign development activities; $18,551,581
  • Education and social justice in the United States: $3,546,076
  • Communication and research: $722,095

The Saint Dominic Foundation is a member of several Catholic international development and emergency service organization umbrella groups, as well as the principal supplier of funds to the Unnachgiebige Kirche in Deutschland: http://www.uk-de.org/
